2. **High-Velocity Execution Architecture**: Celebrating speed and the reality of shipping with AI ("Ship Over Polish", "Prompt-to-Prod" Case Studies, "Ugly Duckling" Showcases).

4. **Bounded Strategy & Decision-Making**: Elevating the founder role to pure executive ("Crossroads" Strategy Boards, CEO Simulator, AI-Augmented Scanning, Crisis Drills, AI-to-AI Pitches).

**Prioritization Results:**

- **Top Priority Theme:** Theme 1: Radical Authenticity & Human Trust.
- **Why It Matters:** In a world overflowing with cheap AI-generated execution, human trust, empathy, and raw authenticity become the only truly scarce and valuable resources. A community that guarantees this trust will be magnetic.

**Action Planning:**

**Idea 1: The "Human Proof-of-Work" Badge & "Vibe Check" Matching**
**Why This Matters:** It immediately establishes trust and provides immense value by ensuring an AI's output actually resonates with humans before launch.

**Next Steps:**
1. **Design a basic Discord/Slack workflow:** Create a #vibe-check channel where founders can drop a link to their latest AI-generated landing page or content.
2. **Establish the "Give and Get" rule:** To receive a vibe check, a founder must first provide one to someone else (enforced by a simple bot or honor system).
3. **Draft the guidelines:** Create a pinned message explaining that technical critiques are forbidden; feedback must *only* focus on "does this feel authentic and clear to a human?"

**Resources Needed:** A Discord or Slack server, a basic ruleset document, and 5-10 founding members to seed the channel activity.
**Timeline:** 1-2 days to setup and invite initial members.
**Success Indicators:** 10+ vibe-check requests fulfilled per week; an increase in founders sharing successful launches that passed the vibe check.

**Idea 2: Honest Post-Mortems Space**
**Why This Matters:** Normalizes the messy reality of AI execution, building deep psychological safety and camaraderie.

**Next Steps:**
1. **Create a specific channel:** Create a #hallucinations-and-failures channel.
2. **Seed the content:** As the community leader, post the first highly embarrassing or catastrophic failure caused by your own agent swarm (e.g., an agent replied to a customer with code instead of English).
3. **Incentivize sharing:** Highlight the "Best Failure of the Week" in a community newsletter to celebrate learning and transparency.

**Resources Needed:** The community platform, personal vulnerability to share the first failure.
**Timeline:** Immediate implementation; weekly highlights.
**Success Indicators:** Consistent weekly sharing of failures; a high ratio of supportive comments and shared learnings in the thread.
